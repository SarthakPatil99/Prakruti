    Frequently Asked Questions

    • What is prakruti?

      The term Prakriti is a Sanskrit word that means, Nature, creativity, or the first creation. 'Pra' means the 'beginning', 'commencement' or 'source of origin' and 'kriti' means 'to form'. When put together, prakriti means 'natural form' or 'original form'. It is the constitution of a body which is determined at the time of conception of the foetus (baby in the womb) and it cannot be changed thereafter. According to ayurveda, Tri-doshas (Vata, Pitta and Kapha) govern creation, maintenance and destruction of bodily tissues as well as the assimilation and elimination. Each person is born with a unique combination of these doshas which decides their basic/genetic constitution called Prakriti. This genetic constitution can be of both "physical body" as well as "mental body".

    • Importance of Prakriti analysis?

      Every body in this universe is composed of basic 5 elements called "pancha mahabhuta". These are: 1. Prithvi(earth) 2. Jala (water) 3. Agni (fire) 4. Vaayu (air) 5. Akash (space) All these are in different ratio in each individual at minutest level. Actually at this minutest level control over the body is not easy. So to get full functional and easy control over the body an upper level of "dosha" is there which is composed of combination of mahabhuta. Type of dosha 1. Kapha(sleshma)= earth + Water 2. Pitta = water + Fire 3. Vaata = air + Space Thus it can be said that these 3 dosha comprises and control whole body. A perfect balance in the tridosha is said to be 'Sama Prakriti', the state of absolute good health. This is a case of rarity. Sama Prakriti is known as the complete state of health. The balanced state of Vaata, Pitta and Kapha results in a state of equilibrium of functions of the body tissues. The vitiation of any one dosha at the level of disease manifestation is counter balanced by another dosha.

    • Factors influencing formation of Prakriti:-

      1. Sperm and Ovum (parental traits) "Pitraja & "Matraja bhaava" Sperm and ovum are two important factors for new life to start. This small cell contains all codes to develop the full grown human body. The code therein is decided by individuals who pass them on to next generation. All behaviour and other mental abilities mark an impression on these genes. Thus these parental traits are passed upon the next progeny through sperms from men and ovum from women. Genetic factors transmitted from father for development of foetus includes factors related to male sexual organs, formation of head and hair on body parts, nail, teeth, bones blood vessels, nerves and predominantly stable elements in the body. Genetic factors transmitted from mother for development of fetus includes factors related female sexual organs, blood, muscles, adipose tissues, nervous tissues, skin, lymphatic, heart, liver, spleen, kidney, gastrointestinal system and predominantly soft tissues and elements in the body. 2. Time of conception Time plays an important role in prakriti formation, as time keeps on changing it becomes very difficult to pinpoint exactly perfect time to conceive, but nevertheless ayurveda gives some hints about what time is best suited for conceiving the baby. This generally comes around 12th to 16th day from 1st day of start of menstrual flow. The time variation according to the seasons, age and clock time also have some effects on the prakriti of the foetus. Details of which has been tabulated here. Cycle of age Childhood- (0 to 16 yrs) kapha predominant Adult-(17 to 69 yrs) pitta predominant Old age- (70 + yrs) vata predominant Cycle of time Early morning / after sunset - kapha predominant Mid day/mid night- pitta predominant Evening / just before sunrise- vata predominant Cycle of seasons Autumn season- kapha predominant Winter season- pitta predominant Rainy season - vata predominant 3. Condition of Uterus Uterus is a place where the new life start, therefore it become utmost necessary to ascertain the condition of uterus. A healthy baby grows only in healthy uterus. Thus when baby develops in healthy uterus its body composition or genes tend to become healthier. In unhealthy uterus the baby becomes more prone to diseases and this problem may persist lifetime. Therefore it can be concluded that condition of uterus also determines the body composition (Prakriti) of the baby. 4. Food and daily routine habits during pregnancy (acquired qualities) "Saatmyaja and Rasaja bhaava" As per Ayurvedic principle each and every food item consists of predominance of Vata, Pitta and Kapha characteristics. When a pregnant lady eats only a particular type of food her body 'dosha' condition changes which in turn affects the prakriti of the baby. Suppose for an example if the pregnant lady eats lots of pickle during her pregnancy, it will result in increase of pitta dosha. This increase in the dosha condition is transferred to the foetus. Thus now the baby will have some traits of pitta dosha also. This logic is also true with the daily regime or daily routine followed by the pregnant lady. 5. Mental state "Satavaj bhaava" Genetic factors transmitted predominantly from parents for development of the mental attributes,of the fetus results in predominance of the qualities of satva, raja and tama guna which is predominant. This results in happiness, sorrow, greed, anger and other emotions, memory and intelligence of the child. 6. Individual unique trait according to "karma” “Atmaja bhaava" Factors transmitted from the "Purush" result in sensory perceptions, knowledge about self, functions associated with mind, life force, etc. at a subtle level Apart from above mentioned factors responsible for the formation of the individual prakriti, one of the most important factor is self! According to the rebirth theory the deeds which remain unfinished in past life, in this life body acquires all the pre-requisite to accomplish the goal.

    • Consideration of unique features:-

      Each and every race or community, etc has its own peculiar manifestation of unique features. All these should be taken in account while determining the "prakriti" of a person. Following common features should be taken into considerations while ascertaining the body constitution - 1. 'Jaati prasakta'- the unique features of the parents' race. 2. 'Kula prasakta' - the unique features of the community. 3. 'Desha anupaatini' - the unique features of his/her country or region of birth. 4. 'Kaalanupatini' - features according to time 5. Vaya anupathini' - features according to age. Every particular group manifest its own traits which are reflected into each individuals of that group. So while analysing the Prakriti of an individual these features should be kept in mind.

    • Types of Prakriti:-

      The prakriti of an individual is divided into basic categories, viz. A. Physical Prakriti B. Mental Prakriti Both physical and mental prakriti are of three types and there could be infinite possibilities of combination of these prakriti. There is a class of people who opine as there are five types of Physical Prakriti viz. 1. Aakash Prakriti 2. Vayu Prakriti 3. Agni Prakriti 4. Jala Prakriti 5. Parthiva Prakriti The above five types are according to the dominance of the Panch mahabhuta present in the body. It is very difficult to get control over these minute elements (Panch Mahabhuta) without disturbing one another. If we try to alter any one mahabhuta, in turn other willalso get disturbed. Thus a level has to be created where we are somewhat at ease to alter the condition arising in our body by disturbances in the balance of the Panch Mahabhutas. Our ancient teachers has formed a level at which it becomes easy to handle the functions in our body, named the Tri dosha. Vata, Pitta and Kapha are three name of tri doshas. Doshas are basically three different set of categories based on the work they perform. 1. Vata (Akash and Vayu) 2. Pitta (Agni and Jal) 3. Kapha (Jal and Prithvi) Thus henceforth everything in ayurveda will now shall be in terms of Vata, Pitta and Kapha. Therefore the classification of the Prakriti is done as following- Types of Physical prakriti:- 1. Vata Prakriti 2. Pitta Prakriti 3. Kapha Prakriti Types of Mental Prakriti 1. Saatvik Prakriti 2. Raajshik Prakriti 3. Taamshik Prakriti
